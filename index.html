About

My work begins with infrastructure. Not as background condition, but as the generative material of architecture — the data networks, flood systems, and logistics structures that sustain cities but remain invisible within them.

At Cal Poly and SCI-Arc, I designed buildings and settlements whose spatial logic derives entirely from these systems: a data center where the helical ramp is the network made walkable; a proto-city for 250,000 organized around water reclamation infrastructure rather than imposed on top of it.

The question I am pursuing is architectural: when infrastructure becomes the building, what kind of public space does it produce?
