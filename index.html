My work begins with infrastructure. Not as background condition, but as the generative material of architecture — the data networks, flood systems, and logistics structures that sustain cities but remain invisible within them. At Cal Poly and SCI-Arc, I designed buildings and settlements whose spatial logic derives entirely from these systems: a data center where the helical ramp is the network made walkable; a proto-city for 250,000 organized around water reclamation infrastructure rather than imposed on top of it. The question I am pursuing is architectural: when infrastructure becomes the building, what kind of public space does it produce?

South Park Data Park

Cal Poly Pomona · Senior Thesis · Spring 2018 · Michael Fox Studio

Los Angeles concentrates more internet exchange infrastructure within a single square mile than almost anywhere on earth. It is also entirely invisible. South Park Data Park proposes a new building type at 12th and Olive: a civic data center where the network becomes architecture — a vertical tower threaded by a public helical ramp, making the invisible exchange infrastructure walkable, legible, and inhabited. The subterranean botanical garden below grade functions as thermal buffer and public counterpoint to the machine above it.

Hydrantis: Irwindale Reimagined

SCI-Arc · Design of Cities · Fall 2021 · Eui-Sung Yi · ★ Exhibited, SCI-Arc Symposium

The San Gabriel River has been managed, channelized, and redirected for 250 years — yet its flood infrastructure remains entirely invisible to the settlements built around it. Hydrantis proposes to reverse this: a proto-city of 250,000 residents whose street grid, housing typology, and public space are derived entirely from the existing flood management system. The city's ground plane is designed for two simultaneous states — normal conditions and the 100-year flood event — so that the infrastructure is never hidden but always inhabited.

Jing Hua Performing Arts Center

SCI-Arc · Spring 2022 · Thom Mayne Studio

Most performing arts centers resolve the conflict between performance and rehearsal by separating them: back-of-house versus front-of-house, servant versus served. Jing Hua refuses this separation. The building is organized around a single continuous folded surface that compresses and expands between the three program states — rehearsal, performance, and the still hours between — so that the boundary between backstage, lobby, and outdoor terrace is not fixed but calibrated to the time of day and the event.

Food Basket Trade Center

Morphosis · International Competition · 2023 · Beijing, China · 59 HA

China's wholesale food infrastructure treats the public as peripheral — warehouse-scale logistics beside, not within, public life. This 59-hectare competition proposal outside Beijing derives its masterplan from bamboo weaving logic: a structural analogy that interlocks cold storage, trading floor, processing, and public market into a continuous section, so that interior and exterior circulate through each other rather than beside each other. Contributed at Morphosis as competition researcher and parametric massing analyst.
